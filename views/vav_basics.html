Basics

A variable air volume (VAV) box is an airflow control device at the zone level. The air coming from the air handler is distributed in to the zone based on its the heating or cooling load. Single duct and dual duct are the two common VAV types. Single duct VAV is widely used in single/multi-zone cooling applications. If heat is needed in the zones, it is supplied at the VAV box through reheat coils (either electric or hot water). Dual duct VAVs are used in systems that have separate hot and cold stream of air delivered to the unit.

The other VAV types are parallel and series fan powered VAV (with and without heating coil). Parallel fan powered VAVs are used when the space has high heating loads. The parallel fan draws in air from the return air plenum. When the fan is off, back draft dampers prevent the primary cool air from entering into the return plenum. Series powered VAVs are suited in zones that need high minimum-air flow rates like a conference room.

VAV box selection is important to ensure overall efficiency of the heating/cooling system. In VAV boxes with reheat coils, this becomes even more important as the reheat coil size impacts the hot water return temperature.
