Types

Cooling Tower Type Flow Advantages Disadvantages Click on image to enlarge 5
Induced Draft
(crossflow)1,2,3
  • Air enters horizontally and water falls vertically through the fill media
  • Hot water basin can be gravity flow
  • Hot water line need not be pressurized, resulting in low pumping head and operational cost
  • Less noise and easy maintenance compared to counterflow
  • Larger tower footprint
  • Large inlet surface makes icing control difficult and sometimes leads to uneven air distribution through the infill
Induced Draft
(counterflow)1,2,3
  • Air flows vertically upwards, opposite to the direction of the water, which falls downwards
  • Smaller tower footprint
  • Efficient air/water contact through droplets formed by spraying
  • Cannot use gravity flow for hot water distribution basin
  • Hot water line needs to be pressurized thus increasing operational cost and noise compared to crossflow
Forced Draft2,4
  • Air is pushed into the cooling tower by a fan located at the base
  • Efficient operation
  • Water distribution and recirculation challenges
Natural Convection2
  • Air is heated by the warm water flowing over the fill. The warm air rises up through convection, pulling in colder air from outside.
  • Low energy consumption because there is no fan
  • Less noise
  • Significantly larger in size compared to induced or forced draft cooling towers
