Types


Cooling tower type Flow Advantage Disadvantage Click on image to enlarge 5
Induced draft
(Crossflow)1,2,3 		· Air enters in the horizontal direction while the water falls vertically through the fill media · Hot water basin can be gravity flow
· Hot water line need not be pressurized thus low pumping head and operational cost
· Lower noise and easy maintenance compared to counter flow 		· Larger tower foot print
· Large inlet surface makes icing control difficult and sometimes leads to uneven air distribution through the infill
Induced draft
(Counterflow)1,2,3		 ·Air flows vertically upwards, opposite to the direction of the water which falls downwards · Smaller tower foot print
· Efficient air/water contact through droplets formed by spraying		 · Cannot use gravity flow for hot water distribution basin
· Hot water line needs to be pressurized thus increasing operational cost and noise compared to crossflow
Forced draft2,4 · Air is pushed into the cooling tower by a fan which is located at the base · Efficient operation · Water distribution and recirculation challenges
Natural convection2 · Air is heated by the warm water flowing over the fill. The warm air rises up through convection pulling in colder air from outside ·Low energy consumption as there is no fan
·Low noise 		· Significantly larger in size compared to induced or forced draft cooling towers
