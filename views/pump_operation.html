Pump maintenance

Periodic pump maintenance is important in order to ensure that the pump is operating as designed. If the pump is not operating near its BEP, the power consumed will be disproportionate to the flow rate. Monitoring is recommended to identify such inefficiencies. The US DOE pumping systems tip sheet #5 gives an outline of pumping systems which includes preventive and predictive actions. It also outlines a checklist for periodic efficiency testing. Preventive action measures consist of coupling alignment, lubrication and seal maintenance and replacement. Predictive action measures are those that help cover for unexpected equipment outage. Vibration analysis, motor current signature analysis, lubrication oil analysis and periodic efficiency testing are methods suggested under predictive actions. The periodic maintenance checklist consists of checking for leakage from packing and mechanical seals, monitoring condition of bearings, evaluating motor/pump alignment and checking motor condition. For more details refer the Energy tips- Pumping system: Maintain pumping systems effectively spreadsheet and Basic pump maintenance chapter of the Improving pumping system performance sourcebook.
