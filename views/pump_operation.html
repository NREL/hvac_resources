Pump Maintenance

If the pump is not operating near its best efficiency point, the power consumed will be high and disproportionate to the flow rate. Monitoring is recommended to identify such inefficiencies.

Pump maintenance measures include:
  • Preventive action measures: Measures taken to prevent a pump from failing, including coupling alignment, lubrication, and seal maintenance and replacement
  • Predictive action measures: Measures that help compensate for unexpected equipment outage, including vibration analysis, motor current signature analysis, lubrication oil analysis, and periodic efficiency testing
  • Periodic maintenance checklist: Checks of packing and mechanical seals for leakage, bearings for general condition, motors/pumps for alignment, and motors for general condition.
For more details refer to:
  • Pumping System Tip Sheet #5, Maintain Pumping Systems Effectively, U.S. Department of Energy, Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy, Industrial Technologies Program.
  • Basic Pump Maintenance chapter (page 33) of the Improving Pumping System Performance: A Sourcebook for Industry.
