Energy Efficiency Measures (EEMs)
“Reducing cooling tower fan speed to 80% of design reduces fan power by 50% while raising cooling tower leaving water temperature only by 3 °F (1.7 °C).”

Saving energy with cooling towers, ASHRAE article

· Operating CT with smaller approach. Year round operating systems reap maximum benefit.

· Install VFD on cooling tower fan. In mild climates, the chiller operates at part load for a major portion of the year.

· Allow condenser water temperatures to drop below traditional 85 F return water to the chiller to improve chiller efficiency. Review total system energy use to determine efficient balance of chiller energy, cooling tower fan energy and pumping energy.

· Consider using cooling tower for “free” water side economizing if chilled water is needed year round. Cooling tower can provide cold water to a heat exchanger to directly cool chilled water without running compressors.

Consider a larger cooling tower to achieve a smaller approach and minimize fan energy. Water Efficiency Measures

Sub-Metering

Sub-metering make-up and blowdown water lines helps to estimate if appropriate amount of water is being used to do the required task.

Increase Cycles of Concentration

Cooling towers typically operate at a blow-down of two to three cycles. This can be increased to upto six or more cycles depending on water quality, presence of automation and constant monitoring. For additional information on how to maximize C.O.C refer 6-13 of WaterSense at Work and page 42 of the Water efficiency manual.

“Increasing the C.O.C from two to six can reduce makeup water consumption by 40%.”

Water Efficiency Manual by N.C. Department of Environment and Natural Resources

Water Quality

Monitor water quality in the cooling tower periodically. Cleansing cooling tower basin, installing filters on side stream taken from cooling tower basin are examples of some recommended measures. Bacterial growths such as Legionella are known to have health concerns due to poor water quality. Controlling sunlight exposure inside the tower surface can significantly reduce biological growth. To read more about methods to improve water quality refer page 6-14 of WaterSense at Work. If there are no recommendations from the manufacturer regarding water quality, refer to Table 12.1: Cooling Water Quality in this document on cooling towers published by Wiley

Design Guide

The first step in designing a cooling tower is to determine the type of cooling tower for the given site and application. Once this is locked down, calculations need to be done to for condenser water flow rate to ensure the cooling tower meets the required capacity. This is done by taking into account the wet-bulb temperature, air velocity and height of the cooling tower. General rule of thumb is 3 gpm of recirculating flow per ton of cooling at 10°F ΔT. Evaporation is estimated at 1.8 gallons/hour per ton of cooling. Follow-up calculation to estimate make-up volume, cycles of concentration and blowdown volume can be found in this article on water use in cooling tower. Few additional things to be specified while designing a cooling tower are

· Frame and casing

· Fill material

· Fill depth

· Nozzles

· Fans

For further information on tower materials refer the Cooling Tower Selection and Sizing document by KLM Technology group.

Cooling tower capacity can be increased without enlarging the physical size by implementing strategies such as installing an efficient spray distribution system and decreasing water temperature.
