Types

The DOE Fundamentals Handbook, Mechanical Science, Volume 2 contains an elaborate module on valves. The list below is a summary of each section in the module. Refer to the handbook for detailed information.

Valve Type Application Advantage Disadvantage Click on image to enlarge
Gate
  • Fully open or closed valve position
  • Low pressure drop compared to globe
  • Limited ability to throttle
  • Long stem length takes up lot of space
Globe
  • Throttling
  • Fully open or closed valve position
  • Better sealing compared to gate valve
  • Long stem length takes up lot of space
Ball
  • Fully open or closed valve position
  • Inexpensive
  • Easy to maintain
  • Poor throttling*
Butterfly
  • On larger pipes
  • Fluids/gases having low pressure and suspended solids
  • Low weight/size
  • Low initial and maintenance cost
  • Pressure difference across valve as some portion of the disk is always exposed to flow
Plug
  • Gas lines
  • Direct flow between different ports
  • Compactness in multiport valve
  • Poor throttling
Diaphragm
  • Start, regulate, stop fluids that should remain contaminant-free
  • Valve not exposed to media in the pipeline
  • Expensive
Check
  • Prevent reverse flow
  • Prevents backflow
  • Maintains pressure
  • Canâ€™t be used on pulsating flows
Solenoid
  • Air systems used to supply air to pneumatic valve actuators
  • Quick action
  • Compact design
  • Signal needs to be continuously on during operation
*Characterized ball valves are good equal percentage valves and are good for throttling. They also have high close-off pressure.
