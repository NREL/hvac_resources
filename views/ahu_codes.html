Air Leakage

““Lowering cabinet air leakage from 5% to 1.5% of system air flow would result in an overall reduction in energy use for space conditioning of about 5% and a reduction of 5% or more energy use for peak heating and cooling loads””

HVAC Cabinet Air Leakage Test Method U.S. DOE

Air leakage in an AHU leads to energy wastage and Indoor Air Quality (IAQ) problems because the AHU is generally located in a Mechanical room which does not have any air quality control measure. ANSI/ASHRAE Standard 193-2010 (RA 2014) outlines the Method of test for determining airtightness of HVAC equipment. The scope of this testing methodology is applicable for equipment moving less than 3000 CFM of air. Furnaces, coil boxes and plenums are the main areas of leakage. The Florida building code Section 13-610.2.A.2.1 Factory Sealed air-handling unit credit, allows an incentive for AHU’s that have been tested and achieved a leakage rate of 2% or less (of the total flow) at a pressure of 1” (water column) and with all air inlets/ports closed.
