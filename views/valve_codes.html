Performance Ratings

Testing standards are important for valves, mainly to understand leakage for a given valve type. Leakage allowances are more critical in the petroleum industry but are also important for central plants. A few standards for valve leakage testing are given below.

American National Standards Institute (ANSI) Fluid Controls Institute (FCI) Standard 70-2: Control Valve Seat Leakage
  • Defines a range of six leakage classes for control valves and their testing procedures
  • Outlines leakage rate in ml/min and bubbles/min for a range of nominal port diameters
  • Most commonly used standard for hot/cold valve applications.
Source: ANSI/FCI 70-2-2013 standard
International Organization for Standardization (ISO) Standard 5208: Industrial Valvesâ€”Pressure testing of metallic valves
  • Outlines different pressure tests and class types for valve types such as globe, plug, check, diaphragm, and butterfly.
  • Leakage rate specified in mm3/sec and drops/sec for different rate types.
Source: ISO 5208: 2008/2015 Standard
American Petroleum Institute (API) Standard 598: Valve inspection and testing
  • No visible leakage permitted
  • Allowable leakage table for liquids and gases depending on valve size
Source: API Standard 598
Manufacturers Standardization Society (MSS) Standard MSS-SP-61: Pressure Testing of Valves
  • Designed mainly for fully open/closed valves, not control valves
  • Leakage rate specified in cc/hr for different nominal pipe diameters.
Source: MSS SP-61-2013
