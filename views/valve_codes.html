Codes and Standards

Testing standards are important for valves, mainly to understand leakage amount of a given valve type. Leakage allowances are more critical in the petroleum industry but nevertheless equally important for central plants. Few standards for valve leakage testing are given below.


ANSI Standard FCI 70-2: Control Valve Seat Leakage by the American National Standards Institute (ANSI)

· Defines a range of six leakage classes for control valves and their testing procedure.

· Outlines leakage rate in ml/min and bubbles/min for a range of nominal port diameters.

· Most commonly used standard for hot/cold valve applications.
Source: ANSI/FCI 70-2-2006 standard



ISO standard 5208: Industrial valves - Pressure testing of metallic valves by the International organization for standardization (ISO)

· Outlines different pressure tests and class types for valve types such as globe, plug, check, diaphragm and butterfly.

· Leakage rate specified in mm3/sec and drops/sec for different rate types.
Source: ISO 5208: 2008/2015 standard



API standard 598: Valve inspection and testing by the American Petroleum Institute (API)

· No visible leakage permitted.

· Allowable leakage table for liquids and gases depending on valve size.
Source: API Standard 598
