Basics
Duct Material Type Application Advantage Disadvantage
Metallic Duct Galvanized Steel Used in air handling system supply and return ducts Strong, rigid, durable, rust-resistant, easy to work (i.e., drill holes, weld, and paint) Poor acoustics, can transmit noise due to vibration; cannot be used in corrosive environment and to handle high temperature fluid
Carbon Steel High temperature duct systems and ducts requiring paint or special coating (flues, stacks, and hoods) Sturdy, rigid, durable, easily available, weldable. and nonporous Gets corroded quickly, heavyweight
Aluminum Clean room, moist air, exhausts Lightweight, corrosion-resistant, lower static friction drop compared to galvanized steel Low strength, expensive compared to galvanized steel, difficult to weld
Stainless Steel Kitchen exhaust, moist air, fume exhaust Corrosion-resistant, can take high polish Difficult to work (i.e., drill holes, bend), expensive compared to galvanized steel
Non metallic Duct Fiberglass- Reinforced Plastic Chemical exhausts, scrubbers, underground duct systems Good sound attenuation, corrosion-resistant High cost, weight, fabrication, code acceptance
Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) Exhaust for chemical fumes and underground duct work Corrosion-resistant, lightweight, easy to work (weld and modify) High cost, inability to handle thermal shock
Polyvinyl Steel (PVS) Ducts, moist air environments Corrosion-resistant, lightweight Inability to withstand high temperatures, prone to coating damage
Flexible Duct Run outs, used to connect supply plenum to diffuser Factory-insulated, fewer duct connections and joints Can get easily torn and crushed, high resistance to airflow

Duct Type Application Advantage Disadvantage
Round Air distribution Smaller pressure drop per unit area, high ratio of air-carrying capacity to pressure drop, less leakage compared to rectangular duct Height cannot be adjusted, needs a fixed amount of clear space
Rectangular Easy fabrication, can fit into tight spaces, provides flat surface for tap-ins Higher pressure drop, uses more material compared to a round duct for same airflow, difficult to seal
Oval Smaller height requirement compared to a round duct Fittings are difficult to fabricate, difficult to handle and ship large sizes
