Basics
Duct material type Application Advantage Disadvantage
Metallic duct Galvanized steel Used in air handling systems- supply and return ducts Strong, rigid, durable, rust-resistant, easy to work (i.e. drill holes, weld and paint) Poor acoustics, can transmit noise due to vibration . Cannot be used in corrosive environment and in handling high temperature fluid
Carbon steel High temperature duct systems and ducts requiring paint or special coating (Flues, stacks and hoods) Sturdy, rigid, durable, easily available, weldable and non-porus Gets corroded quickly, heavy weight
Aluminum Clean room, moist air, exhausts Light weight, corrosion resistant, lower static friction drop compared to galvanized steel Low strength, expensive compared to galvanized steel duct, difficult to weld
Stainless steel Kitchen exhaust, moist air, fume exhaust Corrosion resistant, can take high polish Difficult to work (i.e. drill holes, bend), expensive compared to galvanized steel duct
Non metallic duct Fibreglass reinforced plastic Chemical exhausts, scrubbers, underground duct systems Good sound attenuation, corrosion resistant High cost, weight, fabrication, code acceptance
Polyvinyl chloride (PVC) Exhaust for chemical fumes and underground duct work Corrosion resistant, light weight, easy to work (weld and modify) High cost, inability to handle thermal shock
Polyvinyl steel (PVS) Ducts, moist air environments Corrosion resistant, light weight Inability to withstand high temperatures, prone to coating damage
Flexible duct Run outs, used to connect supply plenum to diffuser Factory-insulated, fewer duct connection, joints Can get easily torn and crushed, high resistance to air flow

Duct type Application Advantage Disadvantage
Round Air-distribution Smaller pressure drop per unit are, high ratio of air-carrying capacity to pressure drop, less leakage compared to rectangular duct Height cannot be adjusted unlike rectangular ducts, needs a fixed amount of clear space
Rectangular Easy fabrication, can fit into tight spaces, provide flat surface for tap-ins Higher pressure drop, use more material compared to a round duct for same air-flow, difficult to seal
Oval Smaller height requirement compared to a round duct Fittings are difficult to fabricate, difficult to handle and ship large sizes
