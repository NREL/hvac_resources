Fan: Procurement and Performance Specifications

With variability in quality and performance between various HVAC equipment manufacturers, it is important to have specifications that define minimum requirements beyond basic capacity. Specifications that include features that allow for appropriate maintenance of equipment will help operations teams sustain efficient performance.

For example, proper and easy access to clean coils, change filters, and check system operation will cut long term cost of ownership. Easy access is not always provided by HVAC manufactures on base equipment.

In addition to basic specification of the HVAC equipment, these systems have options available depending on application. Typical options that should be considered during design and specification include:

  • Humidification
  • Dehumidification
  • High efficiency filtration
  • Air flow measuring station
  • Air side economizing
  • Advanced controls for fault detection and diagnostics (FDD)
  • Safeties and alarms
  • Energy recovery systems
  • Sound attenuation

Resources

  • Fan Efficiency Codes and Standards, Air Movement and Control Association International, Inc., 2013
    Description of codes and standards relevant to HVAC fan selection including AMCA Standard 205 and ASHRAE 90.1.
  • Fan Arrangements, Rotation, Discharge and Motor Position, Fan Engineering: FE-3900, Aerovent
    Descriptions of fan arrangements, rotation, discharge, and motor position using standard industry notations.
  • Fan Efficiency Requirements for Standard 90.1-2013, by John Cermak and Michael Ivanovich, ASHRAE Journal, April 2013, reprinted with permission by AMCA International
    Description of AMCA Standard 205 for characterizing fan efficiency and how it is incorporated into ASHRAE 90.1-2013.
