Pump: Performance Ratings

In a new building, pump performance testing is done to ensure that the pump is operating per the design intent. For existing buildings, the goal is to identify opportunities to improve operations and efficiency. The key components monitored during testing are:

  • Input power
  • Pump status
  • Pump speed (rpm)
  • Differential pressure across the pump

For additional information on pump testing (steps, precautions, and data analysis), refer to Functional Testing for Pumping Systems. This resource includes testing guidance sheets and sample test forms from multiple organizations.

Commonly Used Terms

NPSH

Net positive suction head (NPSH) is the minimum pressure needed to avoid cavitation. NPSHA is the net positive suction head available and NPSHR is the net positive suction head required. NPSHA should be greater than the vapor pressure of the operating fluid. NPSHR is specified by the pump manufacturer. NPSHA should be greater than NPSHR. NPSH analysis is important, especially when sizing a condensate pump. For more details about calculating NPSH, refer to NPSH: A Critical Consideration When Selecting Condensate Transfer Pumps.

Total Pump Head Pressure
