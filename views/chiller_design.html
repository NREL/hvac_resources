Design Guides and Efficiency Measures (EEMs)

Chilled Water Plant Design Guide written by Taylor Engineering as part of the CoolTools from Energy Design Resources. This guide provides a complete overview of setting up a chilled water plant from the design process through procurement and commissioning. The guide is written for engineers who design, redesign, or retrofit chilled water plants. The guide also provides a good introduction and overview for owners, facility managers, and operators.

The guide is written for engineers who design, redesign, or retrofit chilled water plants. The guide also provides a good introduction and overview for owners, facility managers, and operators.

The guide proposes a life cycle cost based approach of selecting chillers. These steps include

1. Calculating the cooling load to be met by the central plant.

2. Short listing chiller manufacturers based on experience from previous installations and local representation.

3. Requesting chiller bids based on performance specifications.

4. Incorporating first cost impact into the bids.

5. Estimating energy usage of each chiller in the bid through a detailed computer model of the building/plant.

6. Evaluating maintenance cost for each manufacturer.

7. Calculating life-cycle cost analysis.

8. Selecting chiller with the lowest life-cycle cost analysis.
