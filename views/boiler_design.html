Energy efficiency measures and design guides
Best practices for boiler energy efficiency

  • Modulate quantity of combustion air: Excess or deficiency of air should be avoided by periodically checking the air to fuel ratio. Refer to the Check burner air to fuel ratio tipsheet
  • Clean scale and soot formation in the boiler: Scale and soot formation act as an insulator reducing the heat transfer efficiency and increasing the flue gas exhaust temperature
  • Install waste heat recovery: Waste heat recovery should be installed after ensuring that the boiler is cleaned and tuned
  • Reduce blowdown cycles and recover waste heat from blow down: Determine boiler blowdown cycle by considering boiler type, operating pressure, water treatment and quality of make-up water. Consider an automatic blowdown control system. Refer to the ‘Minimize Boiler Blowdown ’ tip sheet for more details
  • Preheat combustion air: Wherever possible, consider designing the flue exhaust gas duct such that the heat can be used to preheat the incoming combustion air. Refer to the ‘Preheated combustion air’ tipsheet for more details
Refer Section 9.2 on Boilers in the Operations & maintenance best practices document .

Design guidance
Modular boilers versus large boiler design

A challenge faced by engineers many times is in choosing a boiler configuration for the heating system. Typically, heating systems have two boilers, one to meet the heating load and the second as a back-up if the first boiler fails. Alternatively, multiple small boilers can be installed with redundancy and such a configuration is called modular boiler system.

Advantages of modular boilers Advantages of large boiler
Can efficiently serve varying loads Boilers are compact in size even for large capacities and can take up less space than multiple boilers
Easy to repair or replace a failed boiler without interrupting supply to the building Low initial cost in some applications
Allows cold-start and eliminates standby losses. Jacket losses eliminated from standby boiler
More flexible towards meeting capacity variations
Can easily be transported due to smaller size, reduces installation cost

Disdvantages of modular boilers Disadvantages of large boiler
Adds complexity in piping layout and boiler control Short-cycling when a boiler is oversized. Large pre and post purge losses
Overall area needed for boiler operation might be significant since each boiler needs separate clearance area Longer warm-up time and start-up losses

What should you consider when installing a modular or a large boiler?
  • Is there a distinct swing in the heating load?
  • If yes, then over what time scale is the swing occurring?
  • If it is over a day, then modular boiler might be a better choice than a large boiler
  • If the heating load is constant then a large boiler might be a better choice.
